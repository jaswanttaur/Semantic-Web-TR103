YouTube began as an innovative idea by three former PayPal employees—Chad Hurley, Steve Chen, and Jawed Karim—who wanted to create a platform where people could easily upload and share videos. They founded YouTube in February 2005, and the first video titled “Me at the Zoo” was uploaded on April 23, 2005. The platform quickly gained popularity due to its user-friendly interface and the ability for anyone to share their content with a global audience. The growth of YouTube was rapid, and it caught the attention of tech giant Google, which acquired the platform in November 2006 for US$1.65 billion
