There are standards used to represent relationships with data and web.
Let's take an example of Swiggy:
- One person orders the food.
- One person makes the food. The person making it does not know the one ordering, and the one ordering does not know the cook. Similarly, the delivery person is unfamiliar with both.
- All three are in relationships.

- Similarly, thousands of relationships are formed.

RDF - Triple (form of graph): A general framework for representing interconnected data on the web.

RDF statements are used for describing and exchanging metadata, which enables standardized exchange of data based on relationships. RDF is used to integrate data from multiple sources.

Why are we studying RDF?
It’s fast and efficient, so we are studying it.

Metadata exchange (most important): Exchange data with user and internet.

Judging the behavior of the user:
Every page has different metadata (like a website shows different options for everyone based on their search needs and wants, which are identified based on search history).

Semantic metadata is the description of the meanings of values and of the names of data components, at least for human reference and possibly for programmatic use as well.

Subject - The subject is typically a resource or concept.
Object - The object is the value of the predicate.
Predicate - The predicate is a property or attribute of the subject (makes relationships between subject and object).
